Skin Cancer Facts

About two-thirds of Australians are diagnosed with skin cancer at age 70. Non-melanoma skin cancer is more common in men, with an incidence almost double that of women (Cancer Council Australia, 2019).

Melanoma is the third most common cancer in Australia except for non-melanoma skin cancer. 13,694 Australians were diagnosed with melanoma in 2015 (Cancer Council Australia, 2019).



Every year in Australia (Cancer Council Australia, 2019):

  • About 80% of all newly diagnosed cancers are skin cancers.
  • Most skin cancers are caused by exposure to the sun.
  • The general practitioner has more than 1 million patients consulting skin cancer every year.
  • Around 2000 Australians die each from skin cancer every year.

In 2016, 1960 people died of skin cancer, 1281 from melanoma and 679 from non-melanoma skin cancer in Australia (Cancer Council Australia, 2019).
