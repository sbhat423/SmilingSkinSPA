PREVENTING SKIN CANCER

When the Ultraviolet (UV) radiation level is 3 and above, we recommend the following measures to be taken separately or together to ensure you are protected from the sun:

  • Wear sun-protective clothes to cover your skin. Try to cover as much skin as possible.

  • Use sunscreen. We recommend a sunscreen of broad spectrum with waterproof features and having SPF30 and above. Apply it 20 minutes before going out.

  • Wear a hat. It helps to protect your face, neck and ears.

  • Look for shaded areas where you can hide under.

  • Wear sunglasses when you are outside. Ensure that it meets the Australian standards.

Caution: Please be very careful on days where the Ultraviolet intensity is at its highest. On days like these, it is best to stay indoors.

WHAT IS THE BEST TYPE OF SUNSCREEN?

To ensure that you get the best possible protection when you are in Australia, please follow our strict guidelines to using sunscreen in Australia:

  • Using sunscreen with the highest SPF. Currently in Australia, the highest is SPF50+.

  • Your sunscreen should be broad-spectrum. Meaning it will protect you from both kinds of Ultraviolet (UV) rays, UVA and UVB.

  • Applying the right amount of sunscreen. We found that we only use about 25% to 50% of the required amount of sunscreen. Using too little sunscreen reduces the sun protection factor significantly.

  • All exposed skin must be thoroughly covered with sunscreen.

  • If you are doing water related activities, ensure that you use a waterproof sunscreen and reapply it at least 2 hours regularly.
