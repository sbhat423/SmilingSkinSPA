Treatment for skin cancer

The most common treatment for skin cancer is surgical removal of cancer cells (usually under local anesthesia). Common skin cancer can be treated with ointment or radiotherapy. Skin cancer can also be removed by cryotherapy (using liquid nitrogen to quickly freeze cancer cells), curettage (scraping) or cauterization (burning). Skin cancer is almost always removed. In more advanced skin cancers, some surrounding tissues may also be removed to ensure that all cancer cells are removed.
