Skin Cancer facts and statistics

About two-thirds of Australians are diagnosed with skin cancer at age 70. Non-melanoma skin cancer is more common in men, with an incidence almost double that of women. Excluding non-melanoma skin cancer*, melanoma is Australia's third most common cancer. In 2015, 13,694 Australians were diagnosed with melanoma.
Every year in Australia:

  • Skin cancer accounts for about 80% of all newly diagnosed cancers.
  • Most skin cancers are caused by exposure to sunlight.
  • Global Positioning System (GPS) consults more than 1 million patients with skin cancer every year.
  • The incidence of skin cancer is the highest in the world, two to three times that of Canada, the United States and the United Kingdom.
  • More than 2000 Australians die each from skin cancer every year.


In 2016, 1960 people died of skin cancer, 1281 from melanoma and 679 from non-melanoma skin cancer in Australia.
Although skin cancer is more likely to occur in the elderly, it is important to remember that melanoma is the most serious skin cancer and is a common cancer among young people.

*Non-melanoma skin cancers are not notified to cancer registries.
